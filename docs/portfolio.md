## Interview Questions

1. How do you handle a situation where two parts of a project don't work together?
2. How do you decide which problem to solve first when you have several at the same time?
3. How do you make sure everyone in a team stays on the same page when working on related tasks?
4. How do you explain a technical decision to people who are not involved in the implementation?
5. How do you make sure a piece of code works correctly before connecting it to the rest of the project?
6. What do you do when a task turns out to be much bigger than you expected?
7. How do you approach debugging when a system fails and you have no idea where the problem is?
8. When you have to choose between two valid technical solutions, how do you make the decision?
9. How do you handle a situation where your initial approach turns out to be wrong?
10. What do you do when you depend on another team's work and they are behind schedule?
11. How do you balance speed and quality when you are under time pressure?
12. How do you make sure a tool or library you choose today will still be the right choice later?
13. What is the most difficult thing you have had to learn during a project, and how did you learn it?
14. How do you react when a colleague disagrees with your technical choice?
15. How do you know when a solution is good enough to move on?
